What are "cookies", web beacons, and how does VRDog use them?

VRDog uses computer "cookies" (small files placed on your hard drive) to make your shopping experience more efficient, convenient and personalized. Cookies are alphanumeric identifiers that enable our systems to recognize your browser. Through the use of these cookies, VRDog may automatically collect non-personal information and data, such as your IP address, Internet service provider, location, language, your computer software types and versions, screen resolution and colors, domain name, how you got to our Site (including key words used in a search engines), whether you are a returning or new visitor, how often you visit the Site, and specific VRDog web pages through which you click and how long you spend on each page.

Cookies are not required for you to browse our Site; however, if you don’t accept Cookies, you may not be able to access all portions or features of the Services. Cookies do not contain any PII. They are not used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. The Help portion of the toolbar on most browsers will tell you how to prevent your browser from accepting new cookies, how to have the browser notify you when you receive a new cookie, or how to disable cookies altogether. However, cookies allow you to take full advantage of some of our Site's features, and we recommend that you leave them turned on.

A web beacon (also known as a pixel tag or clear GIF) is a graphic image which is used to better understand the browsing patterns of visitors to the page. Web beacons may be included in our web pages and e-mail messages. We may use cookies and web beacons for insight with respect to the usage of the Site, tracking and monitoring advertising effectiveness, so that we could continually improve our customer’s experience. At times, we use third-parties to analyze usage of our website. However, we do not disclose your personally identifiable information to such third-parties.
